in-house community guidelines

in-house thrives on open, anonymous dialogue. to keep our house clean and conversations constructive, we’ve set a few ground rules. think of them as the shared understanding that keeps our community strong.

keeping it real

  • honesty is key: share your genuine thoughts and experiences. this is a safe space for unfiltered, constructive feedback.
  • respect the anonymity: never try to identify yourself or anyone else. the power of in-house comes from the freedom to speak without fear.

keeping it clean

  • no personal attacks: focus on issues, not individuals. criticize ideas, not people.
  • no hate speech or discrimination: in-house is a place for everyone. bigotry of any kind will not be tolerated.
  • no harassment or bullying: treat everyone with respect, even when you disagree.
  • keep it professional: avoid overly casual or unprofessional language. while honesty is encouraged, maintain a respectful tone.
  • no spam or self-promotion: in-house is not a platform for advertising. focus on workplace discussions and feedback.

keeping it constructive

  • focus on solutions: while identifying problems is important, try to offer potential solutions or suggestions for improvement.
  • be specific: vague complaints are less helpful. provide concrete examples and details to support your feedback.
  • stay on topic: keep discussions relevant to the workplace or community. avoid unrelated or off-topic conversations.

by participating in in-house, you agree to these guidelines. let’s work together to make this a valuable space for everyone.
